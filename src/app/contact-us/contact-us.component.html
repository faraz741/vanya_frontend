FAQ

Our approach at Vanya combines AI, remote sensing, blockchain, and satellite data to precisely measure and verify carbon sequestration on agricultural lands. This data-driven method ensures the accuracy and transparency of carbon credits generated.

Farmers and landowners can earn additional income through carbon credits generated by sustainable agricultural practices. By partnering with us, you'll have access to the tools and support necessary to transition to climate-friendly farming and land management.

AI plays a crucial role at Vanya in analyzing complex climate data, monitoring carbon sequestration, and predicting environmental changes. It enhances the efficiency and accuracy of our carbon credit generation process.

We employ blockchain technology at Vanya to maintain a secure, immutable record of all carbon sequestration data and carbon credits generated. This ensures the integrity and transparency of our carbon credits, building trust among all stakeholders.

Remote sensing and satellite data provide real-time, accurate, and comprehensive data on land use, vegetation, and carbon storage. These technologies enable us to monitor large areas efficiently and ensure the precise quantification of carbon sequestration.

NGOs and corporates can collaborate with us at Vanya in various ways, including funding support for farmers transitioning to sustainable practices, buying carbon credits generated, or implementing our technologies for their own sustainability goals. We're open to discussions on potential partnerships.

We provide comprehensive support at Vanya, including access to our technology, guidance on sustainable practices, and assistance in monitoring and verifying carbon sequestration. Our goal is to make the transition as smooth as possible for farmers and landowners.

To start earning carbon credits, reach out to us via our website or contact form at Vanya. We will guide you through the process, from assessing the potential of your land for carbon sequestration to implementing sustainable practices and earning credits.
